========================================================================
IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE: Social Good 01 App
========================================================================

Follow this exact step-by-step process to implement your project in Snowflake 
and submit it for the hackathon.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1: Deploying the Backend (Data & ML Compute)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log in to your Snowflake Trial Account.
2. On the left-hand navigation menu, click 'Data', then click 'Marketplace'.
3. Search for the exact phrase: "COVID-19 Epidemiological Data" (published by Starschema).
4. Click the blue 'Get' button. Keep the database name as 'STARSCHEMA_COVID19' and confirm. (This instantly mounts the data to your account).
5. On the left-hand menu, click 'Projects' -> 'Worksheets' -> '+ icon' (top right) to create a new SQL Worksheet.
6. Open the '01_infrastructure_setup.sql' file on your computer, copy all the text, and paste it into the Snowflake worksheet.
7. Click the blue 'Run All' button. Let it run for ~30 to 60 seconds. 
*Note: This creates your HACKATHON database and physically trains your ML model in the background.*


------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2: Deploying the Frontend (Streamlit Dashboard)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. On the left-hand menu, click 'Projects' -> 'Streamlit'.
2. Click the '+ Streamlit App' button (top right).
3. Name it "Epidemic Forecasting", select Warehouse: 'COMPUTE_WH', and ensure the App Location is Database: 'HACKATHON' and Schema: 'DATA'. Click 'Create'.
4. The Streamlit editor will open with some sample code. Delete all of it.
5. Open your 'ml_ai_app.py' file on your computer, copy all the text, and paste it into the Snowflake Streamlit editor.
6. Click the blue 'Run' button. Your dashboard will instantly render the charts and LLM summaries!


------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3: The Video Demo (Crucial for Judging!)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open up OBS, Loom, or Zoom and record your screen.
2. Start the video and explain why you chose this architectural design. 
3. Key talking points to secure 1st place:
   - Show the SQL worksheet and explain that you pre-calculated 7-day rolling averages at the data layer to eliminate weekend reporting noise (shows high Quality).
   - Show how fast the frontend loads and explain it is because you decoupled the SNOWFLAKE.ML.FORECAST algorithm to the backend (shows Technical Depth).
   - Show the dashboard, specifically point out the MAPE score, and show how the Cortex LLM (snowflake-arctic) adapts its confidence based on that stat.
   - Show the "Methodology & Fairness" tab and talk about testing bias (shows Real-World Impact).
4. Upload the video to YouTube as "Unlisted".


------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4: Final GitHub Submission
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open the 'README.md' file located in your desktop SNWFLAKE_HACKATHON folder.
2. Replace [INSERT_YOUTUBE_OR_LOOM_LINK_HERE] with your actual YouTube link.
3. Push the entire folder to a public repository on your GitHub account.
4. Submit that GitHub URL to the Hackathon submission page!
